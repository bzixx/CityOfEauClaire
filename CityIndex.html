These businesses have made a name for themselves Nationwide and have a large Employment presence in the Chippewa Valley

  2. Uniroyal Tire, now American Phoneix
  3. JAMF Software
  4. HSHS Sacred Heart Hostpital

Image of Business in Eau Claire Description

Menards Employment Center and Corporate Operations in North Eau Claire.

The forerunner of Uniroyal, Inc.'s Eau Claire Plant was the Gillette Safety Tire Company, founded in Eau Claire in 1916 by Raymond B. Gillette of Michigan, an inventor of a safety shoe for tires. S.P. Woodard of the Car Spring Rubber Company served as the company's first president; Gillette, first vice president and general manager. The plant engineer was R.W. Hutchens. Approximately 250 people were employed at the plant which produced 200 rubber tires and 200 inner tubes per day.

JMAF's humble beginnings were born in the computer lab at the University of Wisconsin - Eau Claire when our co-founder, Zach Halmstad, created Casper Suite (now known as Jamf Pro). And the rest, as they say, is history. Ever since, JAMF has lived Zach’s vision to create more efficient ways to deploy, manage and protect Apple devices.

HSHS Sacred Heart's history dates back to 1889, two Hospital Sisters of St. Francis settled in Eau Claire. The first hospital was started in a house on Putnam.

Opening in 1987 as a co-manufacturing site with Land O’Lakes, the facility produced Nestlé formulas like Carnation Good Start and Follow Up until Nestlé purchased the plant in 1990

Royal Credit Union was started in April of 1964 by employees of the Uniroyal Tire Company in Eau Claire, Wisconsin. That first year, there were just 100 Royal Credit Union Members between the employees and their families. By November of 1967, Royal had 2,100 Members and surpassed $1 million in assets
